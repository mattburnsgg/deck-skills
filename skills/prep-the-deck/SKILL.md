Interview the user relentlessly about this deck until you both reach a locked brief. This is the grill. A deck built on a vague brief is a deck rebuilt three times.

## Rules of the grill
(Adapted from Matt Pocock's `grill-me`.)
- Ask ONE question at a time. Wait for the answer before the next.
- For every question, give your recommended answer and a one-line why.
- If a question can be answered by research (reading a URL, a doc, the existing deck), research it instead of asking.
- Keep going until every branch below is resolved. Do not start designing slides mid-grill.

## The decision tree
Walk it in order. Later answers depend on earlier ones.

1. **The one outcome.** When this deck ends, what does the audience do, decide, or feel? If there is no single outcome, find it before anything else.
2. **Audience and register.** Who is in the room? What do they already know? What do they care about? Investor, customer, team, conference, sales, and teaching each change everything downstream.
3. **Format and venue.** A live talk you present, a recorded video or explainer, or a send-as-link they read alone? This sets slide count, density, and whether you write a separate speaker script.
4. **Length.** Time budget or slide count. Map it to the format from question 3.
5. **The thesis.** State the whole deck in one sentence. If you cannot, keep working until you can. The thesis is the spine.
6. **The arc.** Name the through-line. Problem to solution, before to after, a hero's journey, a countdown, a ranked list. Pick one.
7. **Must-include and must-cut.** The non-negotiable points, and what you are deliberately leaving out.
8. **Proof.** What real data, charts, screenshots, quotes, or examples back the claims? Where do they live? Can each one be captured from a source? No invented data, ever.
9. **Visual treatment.** Real captured charts and cards only, never hand-drawn graphs. Confirm the legibility bar (readable from the back of the room). Capture the brand: colors, fonts, logo, any banned words.
10. **Tone references.** Decks, talks, or sites they love. Steal the cadence, not the content.
11. **Reuse vs fresh.** Existing assets to pull from, or a clean build.

## Land the brief
When every branch is resolved, write a one-page **deck brief**:
- outcome, audience, format, length, thesis, arc
- the slide-by-slide beat list, with the proof that anchors each beat
- the visual rules (brand tokens, legibility floor, real-charts-only)

Read it back. Get a clear yes. Then hand to `build-the-deck`.

The grill is the most important step. Skipping it is why decks get rebuilt.
